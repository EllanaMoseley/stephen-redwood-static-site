Stephen is an award winning director, actor and writer.

Highlights of his work include playing Puck in the BBC Scottish Symphony Orchestra’s A Midsummer Night’s Dream broadcast on Radio 3, working with Daniel Portman in Reaper, taking Keep The Home Fires to the Edinburgh Fringe, voicing Kaylish in Paragon and devising with Graham McLaren on Kidnapped.

When not acting, he can be found on a trampoline, or halfway up a mountain and he relishes the challenge of learning something completely new.

He is a Grade 8 pianist and a proficient singer, often working in musical theatre as well as other stage work. He is an adept composer, writer and director, recently directing Charlie at the Edinburgh Student Theatre Festival which won the award for Best Performance.

He is a graduate of the Traverse Theatre’s Young Writers Program, is currently part of the Book, Music and Lyrics musical theatre writing development program and the Soho Young Playwrights.

He trained at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland, prior to which, he attended Guildford School of Acting on their foundation course. He is the Artistic Director of Crimson Phoenix, with which he creates his own work.

He is a morning person, much to the chagrin of his flatmates.

Full credits can be found on his Spotlight CV and in the relevant sections of this website.
